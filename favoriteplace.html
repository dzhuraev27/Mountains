Your Favorite Places

Halla Mountain (한라산)

Located on Jeju Island, Halla Mountain is the highest peak in South Korea, renowned for its rich natural beauty and unique terrain

Seoraksan (설악산)

Situated in Gangwon Province, Seoraksan is famous for its breathtaking scenery, often referred to as the beauty of the Orient.

Jirisan (지리산)

Spanning across Jeollanam-do and Gyeongsangnam-do, Jirisan is the second-highest mountain in South Korea and is designated as a national park.

Deokyusan (덕유산)

Located in Jeollabuk-do, Deokyusan is the highest mountain in western South Korea, offering beautiful landscapes that change with the seasons.

Taebaeksan (태백산)

Stretching across Gangwon Province and Chungcheongbuk Province, Taebaeksan offers panoramic views and winter sports activities.

Wolchulsan (월출산)

Located in Jeollanam-do, Wolchulsan boasts picturesque landscapes, including famous cloud bridges and rocky formations.

Odaesan (오대산)

Famous for its spring cherry blossoms and autumn foliage, Odaesan in Gangwon Province is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts.

Baekdudaegan (백두대간)

The backbone mountain range of the Korean Peninsula, featuring several prominent peaks and diverse ecosystems.

Sobaeksan (소백산)

Part of the Gyeryongsan mountain range, Sobaeksan offers beautiful hiking trails and stunning views in Chungcheongbuk-do.

Chiaksan (치악산)

Designated as a national park, Chiaksan in Gangwon Province is known for its rugged terrain and diverse plant life.

Daeunsan (대둔산)

One of the tallest mountains, straddling Chungcheongbuk-do and Gyeongsangbuk-do, Daeunsan offers challenging hikes and panoramic vistas.

Naejangsan (내장산)

Located in Jeollabuk-do, Naejangsan is famous for its vibrant autumn foliage, attracting many visitors each fall.

Gyeryongsan (계룡산)

Situated between Daejeon and Chungnam, Gyeryongsan is a popular hiking destination with historical significance.

Jangansan (장안봉)

A mountain near Seoul, Jangansan provides a convenient escape for urban dwellers, offering scenic views.

Gayasan (가야산)

Spanning Gyeongsangnam-do and Gyeongsangbuk-do, Gayasan holds historical and cultural significance, featuring ancient temples.

Cheonjeongsan (천등산)

Overlooking the East Sea in Yangyang-gun, Gangwon-do, Cheonjeongsan offers breathtaking coastal views.

Gwanaksan (관악산)

Easily accessible from Seoul, Gwanaksan is a popular mountain for city residents, providing a natural retreat.

Mudeungsan (무등산)

Located in Gwangju and Jeollanam-do, Mudeungsan is known for its unique rock formations and diverse ecosystems.

Yongmunsan (용문산)

Found in Paju, Gyeonggi-do, Yongmunsan offers a serene hiking experience near Seoul.

Sokrisan (속리산)

Positioned in Sokcho, Gangwon-do, Sokrisan offers stunning views of the East Sea, making it a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers.
